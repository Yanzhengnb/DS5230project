New Ideas from Data Insights

One critical insight is that different authors in high fantasy are defined by how they use fantasy names and linguistic features. For example, a distinguishing feature of Brandon Sanderson is heavy use of complex invented character and place names, while authors like Ursula K Le Guin and George R R Martin have high passive voice ratios. This suggests that a Sanderson novel contains more active scenes with character actions and development while a Game of Thrones novel uses more scene and environmental descriptions. Additionally, authors like Sanderson may rely more on smaller subsets of characters with high story influence while Le Guin and Martin may have a wider array of characters with smaller overall influence on the story.

This points to a new direction of investigation which may be useful in distinguishing styles within the same subgenre while identifying similar styles between subgenres: analysis of character distribution. Determining how authors engage with world-building could inform relationships between different authors with similar writing styles across various subgenres.

Proposed Scope for the Final Report

Based on preliminary results, we propose some modifications to the scope of the project. Primary focus will be on analysis of clusters developed within subgenres to determine how authors writing in similar spaces differ and how authors in different subgenres are similar.

One additional area worth investigating is topic modeling. While likely not applicable to the entire dataset, it will likely produce insights which help understand outliers. Additionally it can be implemented to investigate 1-2 individual clusters more deeply to see how authors align in content as well as style.

Conclusion

Our preliminary results suggest that increasing the volume of text analyzed will be the best way to determine the quality of learning to be done. With only a dataset of 9 novels the clusters and insights generated are low quality and of limited use. However, we have found some significant insights including a similarity of sentiment and tone as well as significantly differing sentence length and passive voice usage. These insights indicate that our analysis will produce more significant conclusions as we scale up our dataset.
